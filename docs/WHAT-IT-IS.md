# What Kingdom Come Is

Kingdom Come is a tool for schools that train people to be ministers, pastors, and church leaders.

It helps teachers see how their students are *really* doing — not just on tests, but in their growth as whole people.

## Why does this matter?

A student might look fine in class but be quietly struggling. Maybe they stopped writing in their reflection journal. Maybe they haven't shown up to their small group in two weeks. By the time anyone notices, it can be too late to reach out.

Kingdom Come watches for these quiet warning signs and tells the teacher *early*, while there's still time to help.

## What it does

- **Notices when a student is drifting.** It looks at signs like how engaged someone has been and whether they've been writing reflections, and gives the teacher a heads-up.
- **Suggests what to study next.** Based on what a student feels called to do (preach, care for families, lead worship, and so on), it points them at the right next reading or practice.
- **Helps with small-group planning.** Teachers can drag students into groups, and the app will flag groups that are too small or unbalanced.
- **Keeps a record of real-world good work.** When a student does something meaningful — leads a community meal, starts a youth night, mentors someone — the teacher writes it down in one click. Over time this becomes a real story of how the student is growing.
- **Gives each student a calm AI mentor to talk to.** Like a thoughtful friend who remembers what you've talked about before. Students can bring questions, doubts, or things they're wrestling with, and get a warm, short reply.

## Who uses it

Two kinds of people:

- **Marcus** is a student in training. He opens the app in the morning and sees one card: how he's doing this week, what to focus on, and a button to talk to his mentor.
- **Sister Theresa** is his teacher (the school calls her a "formation director"). She opens the app and sees her whole class at a glance — who's thriving, who's steady, who needs a check-in this week, and who's at risk.

## Who it's for

Schools that train ministers (called seminaries), church networks that train leaders, and any team that wants to move from messy spreadsheets to something that actually helps them notice and care for the people they're responsible for.

## What's special about it

- It's **free and open source.** Anyone can read the code, use it, change it, and share it.
- It's built to feel calm and thoughtful, like a quiet study room — not loud and busy like most apps.
- It treats students as people, not numbers. Every warning comes with a plain-English reason ("Marcus hasn't reflected in nine days") instead of a score.
